Present Simple Tense - Practice

Introduction

The present simple tense is used to talk about regular actions, facts, habits, and general truths. This quiz will help you practice using the present simple correctly.

Remember these key points:

  • We add -s or -es to the verb for third person singular (he/she/it)
  • We use do/does to make questions and negatives
  • The present simple is often used with time expressions like: always, usually, often, sometimes, never, every day, etc.

Understanding the Present Simple Tense

The present simple has four main uses:

  1. Regular habits or routines: I walk to work every day.
  2. Permanent situations: My parents live in London.
  3. General truths and facts: The sun rises in the east.
  4. Timetables and schedules: The train leaves at 6 pm.

Native speaker note: Native speakers often use the present simple with stative verbs (like, love, hate, believe, etc.) rather than continuous forms: "I believe you" (not "I am believing you").
