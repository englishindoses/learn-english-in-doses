Beginner ESL Grammar Course

Welcome to the Beginner English Grammar Course by English in Doses. This course is designed to help you build a strong foundation in English grammar. You will learn the essential grammar rules step by step, starting from the basics and building up to more complex structures. Each lesson includes clear explanations, simple examples, and practice activities to help you understand and use English grammar correctly.

This course will help you:

  • Build a strong foundation in English grammar.
  • Express yourself clearly in everyday situations.
  • Understand basic English texts and conversations.
  • Prepare for A1 and A2 level English exams.
  • Move confidently to intermediate level English.

Course Structure

Each topic includes:

  1. Introduction - Simple overview of the grammar rule.
  2. Form - How to make the grammar structure.
  3. When to use it - Clear explanations of when to use each grammar rule.
  4. Examples - Simple, clear examples you can understand easily.
  5. Native Speaker Note - How native speakers really use the grammar.
  6. Common Errors - Mistakes to avoid.
  7. Practice Exercises - Test your knowledge with multiple choice questions.
  8. Useful Tips - Extra help to learn and remember the grammar.
  9. Extra Practice - More activities to practice what you have learned.

Course Index

The topics are in order from easiest to hardest. Follow them in order for the best learning experience, or look for specific topics you want to study. Tick the checkboxes to track your progress!

1. Basic Sentence Building (A1)

  • Basic Sentence Structure & Word Order
  • Subject Pronouns (I, you, he, she, it, we, they)
  • Object Pronouns (me, you, him, her, it, us, them)
  • Basic Question Formation (Yes/No and Wh- questions)
  • Imperatives (Commands and Instructions)

2. Present Tense (A1)

  • Present Simple with 'be' (am/is/are)
  • Present Simple with Other Verbs
  • Present Continuous (Actions Happening Now)
  • Present Simple vs Present Continuous (A2)
  • Adverbs of Frequency (always, usually, sometimes, never)

3. Nouns, Articles & Possession (A1)

  • Singular and Plural Nouns
  • Articles (a/an/the)
  • Demonstratives (this/that/these/those)
  • Possessive Adjectives (my, your, his, her, its, our, their)
  • Possessive 's (John's book)
  • Have / Have Got (Possession)

4. Prepositions & Location (A1)

  • There is / There are
  • Prepositions of Place (in, on, under, next to, between)
  • Prepositions of Time (in, on, at, from...to)

5. Basic Verbs & Abilities (A1)

  • Can / Can't (Ability and Possibility)
  • Like / Love / Hate + -ing (Basic Gerunds)

6. Past Tense (A2)

  • Past Simple with 'be' (was/were)
  • Past Simple with Other Verbs (Regular and Irregular)
  • Past Time Expressions (yesterday, last week, ago)
  • Past Continuous (Basic - Actions in Progress in the Past)

7. Comparisons (A2)

  • Comparative Adjectives (-er, more, irregular forms)
  • Superlative Adjectives (-est, most, irregular forms)
  • Comparisons with as...as

8. Quantity & Amount (A2)

  • Countable & Uncountable Nouns (a/an, some, any, much, many)
  • How Much / How Many
  • Too / Enough (with adjectives and nouns)

9. Future Tenses (A2)

  • Future with 'going to' (Plans and Intentions)
  • Future with 'will' (Predictions and Spontaneous Decisions)

10. Modal Verbs (A2)

  • Modal Verbs: 'should' (Giving Advice)
  • Modal Verbs: 'must' / 'mustn't' (Obligation and Prohibition)
  • Modal Verbs: 'have to' / 'don't have to' (Necessity)

11. Linking Words (A2)

  • Basic Linking Words (and, but, because, so, then, after that, finally)

12. Basic Conditionals (A2)

  • Zero Conditional (General Truths and Facts)
  • First Conditional (Real Future Possibilities)

13. Present Perfect (Basic A2)

  • Present Perfect (Basic - Experiences with ever/never)
  • Present Perfect (Recent Actions with just/already/yet)
  • Present Perfect vs Past Simple (Basic Distinction)

Getting Started

Start with module one to follow the complete course structure, or jump to any specific grammar topic you need to study. Make sure you complete the practice activities after each lesson. For extra help and practice, book a private conversation lesson designed just for you.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

  • Use basic English grammar correctly in everyday situations.
  • Make simple sentences with correct word order.
  • Ask and answer basic questions.
  • Talk about the present, past, and future.
  • Express your needs, wants, and opinions clearly.
  • Understand simple English texts and conversations.
