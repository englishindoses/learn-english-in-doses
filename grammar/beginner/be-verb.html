Be Verb (am/is/are)

The verb "be" is the most important verb in English. We use am, is, and are to talk about who we are, where we are, and how we are.

Learning Objectives

In this lesson you will learn to:

  • Use am, is, and are correctly with different subjects
  • Make positive and negative sentences with the be verb
  • Ask and answer questions using am, is, and are

Positive Sentences

We use the be verb to describe people, places, and things.

Structure: subject + am/is/are + ...

Which form to use:

  • am ‚Üí I
  • is ‚Üí he, she, it
  • are ‚Üí you, we, they
  1. i am a teacher.
  2. she is happy.
  3. they are from Spain.

Native speaker note: In speaking, we often use short forms: I'm, he's, she's, it's, we're, you're, they're.

Negative Sentences

We add "not" after the be verb to make negative sentences.

Structure: subject + am/is/are + not + ...

Short forms:

  • am not (no short form)
  • is not = isn't
  • are not = aren't
  1. i am not tired.
  2. he isn't a doctor.
  3. we aren't late.

Native speaker note: We usually use the short forms (isn't, aren't) in speaking and informal writing. The full forms (is not, are not) sound more formal.

Questions

We move the be verb before the subject to make questions.

Structure: am/is/are + subject + ... ?

  1. are you a student?
  2. is she from Italy?
  3. am i late?

Native speaker note: We use short answers: "Yes, I am" or "No, I'm not". We don't usually say the full sentence again in the answer.

Summary of the Be Verb

Subject Positive Negative Question
i i am i am not / i'm not am i?
he/she/it he is / she is / it is he isn't / she isn't / it isn't is he? / is she? / is it?
you/we/they you are / we are / they are you aren't / we aren't / they aren't are you? / are we? / are they?

Common Errors

Watch out for these mistakes!

Error 1: ‚ùå I is a teacher.

Correct: ‚úì i am a teacher.

Use "am" with "I", not "is".

Error 2: ‚ùå They is from China.

Correct: ‚úì they are from China.

Use "are" with "they", not "is".

Error 3: ‚ùå She not is happy.

Correct: ‚úì she is not happy.

Put "not" after the be verb, not before.

Multiple Choice Questions

Choose the correct sentence.

1. Which sentence is correct?

A. I are a doctor.
B. I am a doctor.
C. I is a doctor.
Incorrect. We use "am" with "I", not "are".
Correct! We use "am" with "I".
Incorrect. We use "am" with "I", not "is".

2. Which sentence is correct?

A. Are she from Japan?
B. Is she from Japan?
C. Am she from Japan?
Incorrect. We use "is" with "she", not "are".
Correct! We use "is" with "she".
Incorrect. We use "is" with "she", not "am".

3. Which sentence is correct?

A. We aren't late.
B. We isn't late.
C. We amn't late.
Correct! We use "aren't" (are not) with "we".
Incorrect. We use "aren't" with "we", not "isn't".
Incorrect. "Amn't" is not a real word.

4. Which sentence is correct?

A. He are my friend.
B. He am my friend.
C. He is my friend.
Incorrect. We use "is" with "he", not "are".
Incorrect. We use "is" with "he", not "am".
Correct! We use "is" with "he".

5. Which sentence is correct?

A. You is hungry?
B. Are you hungry?
C. Is you hungry?
Incorrect. We use "are" with "you". Also, in questions the be verb comes first.
Correct! In questions, we put "are" before "you".
Incorrect. We use "are" with "you", not "is".
