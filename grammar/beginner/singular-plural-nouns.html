Singular and Plural Nouns

Introduction

A singular noun means ONE thing. A plural noun means TWO or MORE things. In English, we usually add "-s" to make a noun plural, but there are some special rules and some nouns that change completely. This lesson will teach you the most important rules.

After this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Make regular nouns plural by adding -s or -es.
  • Spell plural nouns correctly with special endings (-y, -f, -fe).
  • Use the most common irregular plurals (child/children, man/men, person/people).

1. Regular Plurals: Add -s

Form: noun + s

When to use it: Most nouns become plural by adding -s at the end.

  1. one book → two books.
    (= I have two books at home.)
  2. one desk → three desks.
    (= There are three desks in the office.)
  3. one ticket → four tickets.
    (= I bought four tickets for the train.)

2. Add -es After -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -z

Form: noun + es

When to use it: When a noun ends in -s, -sh, -ch, -x, or -z, add -es. This makes it easier to say.

  1. one bus → two buses.
    (= Two buses go to the city centre.)
  2. one dish → three dishes.
    (= I washed three dishes after dinner.)
  3. one box → five boxes.
    (= We need five boxes for the trip.)

3. Nouns Ending in -y

Form: consonant + y → change -y to -ies. Vowel + y → add -s.

When to use it: Look at the letter BEFORE the -y. If it is a consonant (b, c, d, etc.), change -y to -ies. If it is a vowel (a, e, i, o, u), just add -s.

Consonant + y → -ies:

  1. one city → two cities.
    (= I visited two cities on my holiday.)
  2. one baby → three babies.
    (= There are three babies in the room.)
  3. one company → many companies.
    (= Many companies are in this building.)

Vowel + y → -s:

  1. one day → seven days.
    (= There are seven days in a week.)
  2. one key → two keys.
    (= I have two keys for the hotel room.)
  3. one boy → four boys.
    (= Four boys are in the park.)

4. Nouns Ending in -f or -fe

Form: change -f / -fe to -ves

When to use it: Some common nouns change -f or -fe to -ves in the plural.

  1. one knife → two knives.
    (= There are two knives on the table.)
  2. one life → many lives.
    (= Doctors save many lives.)
  3. one half → two halves.
    (= I cut the sandwich into two halves.)

Remember: Not all -f words follow this rule. Some just add -s: one roof → two roofs, one chef → two chefs. The most common ones that change are: knife, wife, life, half, leaf, wolf, shelf.

5. Irregular Plurals

When to use it: Some nouns do not follow the normal rules. You need to learn these by memory. Here are the most common ones.

  1. one man → two men. / one woman → two women.
    (= Two men and three women work in my team.)
  2. one child → three children.
    (= She has three children.)
  3. one person → ten people.
    (= Ten people are waiting at the bus stop.)

Some nouns are the same in singular and plural:

  1. one fish → five fish.
  2. one sheep → ten sheep.

Other important irregular plurals: tooth → teeth, foot → feet, mouse → mice.

Summary: Plural Nouns

Rule Singular Plural
Most nouns: + s book, car, desk books, cars, desks
-s, -sh, -ch, -x, -z: + es bus, dish, box buses, dishes, boxes
Consonant + y: -ies city, baby, company cities, babies, companies
Vowel + y: + s day, key, boy days, keys, boys
-f / -fe: -ves knife, wife, life knives, wives, lives
Irregular man, child, person, tooth men, children, people, teeth

Common Errors

Forgetting to add -s

I have two brother.

I have two brothers.

When there is more than one, you must add -s (or the correct plural ending) to the noun.

Adding -s to irregular nouns

The childrens are at school.

The children are at school.

"Children" is already plural. Do not add -s again.

Wrong spelling with -y endings

I visited three countrys.

I visited three countries.

When a noun ends in consonant + y, change -y to -ies. "Country" → "countries".

How to Remember and Practice

📝 Tip: Make flashcards for the irregular plurals. Write the singular on one side and the plural on the other. Test yourself every day.

📝 Tip: When you learn a new noun, always learn its plural at the same time. For example, learn "child — children" together, not just "child".

📝 Tip: Look around you right now. Name things you can see and say the plural. "One chair, two chairs. One window, three windows." This is a quick and easy way to practise.
