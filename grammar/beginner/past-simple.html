Past Simple (Regular and Irregular Verbs)

Introduction

The past simple is the tense you use to talk about finished actions in the past. You use it every day to talk about what you did yesterday, last week, or last year. Some verbs are "regular" (they add -ed) and some are "irregular" (they change completely). This lesson will teach you both.

After this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Make positive sentences in the past simple with regular (-ed) and irregular verbs.
  • Make negative sentences with "didn't".
  • Make questions with "did".

1. Positive Sentences: Regular Verbs

Form: subject + verb + ed

When to use it: Use the past simple for finished actions in the past. Regular verbs add -ed to make the past form.

  1. I worked late yesterday.
    (= My work finished. It was yesterday.)
  2. She cooked dinner for the family.
    (= The cooking is finished.)
  3. We arrived at the hotel at 10 pm.
    (= The arrival is finished.)

Spelling rules for -ed: Most verbs add -ed (worked, cleaned, played). Verbs ending in -e add only -d (lived, arrived, liked). Verbs ending in consonant + y change to -ied (study → studied, carry → carried). Short verbs ending in consonant-vowel-consonant double the last letter (stop → stopped, plan → planned).

2. Positive Sentences: Irregular Verbs

Form: subject + irregular past form

When to use it: Many common verbs are irregular. They do NOT add -ed. You need to learn these by memory.

  1. I went to the supermarket after work.
    (= go → went.)
  2. She had a meeting this morning.
    (= have → had.)
  3. We took a taxi to the airport.
    (= take → took.)

The most common irregular past forms: go → went, have → had, do → did, see → saw, come → came, make → made, take → took, get → got, give → gave, eat → ate, drink → drank, buy → bought, say → said, tell → told, think → thought, know → knew, find → found, leave → left, meet → met.

3. Negative Sentences

Form: subject + didn't + verb (base form, NO -ed, NO irregular form).

When to use it: Use "didn't" (did not) to make any past simple sentence negative. The main verb goes back to the base form.

  1. I didn't watch TV last night.
    (= NOT "didn't watched".)
  2. She didn't go to the party.
    (= NOT "didn't went".)
  3. We didn't have time for lunch.
    (= NOT "didn't had".)

Important: After "didn't", the verb ALWAYS goes back to the base form. This is the same for regular AND irregular verbs. "Didn't" already shows the past, so the main verb does not need to change.

4. Questions

Form: did + subject + verb (base form) + ?

When to use it: Use "Did" at the beginning to make yes/no questions. The main verb is always the base form.

  1. Did you enjoy the trip?
    (= I want to know about your trip.)
  2. Did she call the office?
    (= I want to know if she made a phone call.)
  3. What did you do yesterday?
    (= I want to know about your day.)

Short answers:

  1. Did you like the food? — Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.
  2. Did he come to work? — Yes, he did. / No, he didn't.
  3. Did they arrive on time? — Yes, they did. / No, they didn't.

Remember: After "did", use the base form. Say "Did she go?" (NOT "Did she went?"). "Did" already shows the past.

5. Past Simple of "Be" (was / were)

Form: I / he / she / it + was. You / we / they + were.

When to use it: The verb "be" is special. It does NOT use "did" or "didn't" in the past. It has its own past forms.

  1. I was tired after work.
    (= I felt tired. The feeling is finished.)
  2. The hotel was very nice.
    (= I am describing the hotel in the past.)
  3. They were at the airport at six o'clock.
    (= Their location in the past.)

Negative: was not → wasn't. Were not → weren't.

  1. The food wasn't good.
  2. We weren't happy with the service.

Questions: Put was/were before the subject.

  1. Was the meeting long? — Yes, it was. / No, it wasn't.
  2. Were you at home yesterday? — Yes, I was. / No, I wasn't.

Summary: Past Simple

Type Regular Verbs Irregular Verbs "Be"
Positive I worked. I went. I was. / They were.
Negative I didn't work. I didn't go. I wasn't. / They weren't.
Question Did you work? Did you go? Was he...? / Were they...?
Short answer (+) Yes, I did. Yes, I did. Yes, I was. / Yes, they were.
Short answer (−) No, I didn't. No, I didn't. No, I wasn't. / No, they weren't.

Common Errors

Using -ed or the irregular form after "didn't"

I didn't went to the shop.

I didn't go to the shop.

After "didn't", always use the base form. "Didn't" already shows the past.

Using "did" with "was/were"

Did you was tired?

Were you tired?

"Be" does not use "did" in the past. Use "was" or "were" directly.

Adding -ed to irregular verbs

I eated lunch at one o'clock.

I ate lunch at one o'clock.

"Eat" is an irregular verb. The past form is "ate", not "eated".

How to Remember and Practice

📝 Tip: Every evening, say three things you did today: "I went to work. I had lunch with a friend. I watched a film." This is the best daily practice for the past simple.

📝 Tip: Learn irregular verbs in small groups of five. This week, learn: go/went, have/had, see/saw, come/came, make/made. Next week, learn five more.

📝 Tip: Remember that "didn't" and "did" do all the work. After them, the verb is ALWAYS the base form. Think of "did" as carrying the past meaning so the verb doesn't have to.
