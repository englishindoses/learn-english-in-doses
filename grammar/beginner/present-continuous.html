Present Continuous

Introduction

The present continuous (also called "present progressive") describes things that are happening RIGHT NOW or around now. If someone asks you "What are you doing?", you answer with the present continuous. It is different from the present simple, which talks about habits and routines.

After this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Make positive, negative, and question sentences in the present continuous.
  • Spell verb-ing forms correctly.
  • Understand the difference between present simple and present continuous.

1. Positive Sentences

Form: subject + am / is / are + verb-ing

When to use it: Use the present continuous for actions happening now, at this moment.

  1. I am eating lunch right now.
    (= I am in the middle of eating.)
  2. She is working at her desk.
    (= She is at work now, doing her job.)
  3. We are waiting for the bus.
    (= We are at the bus stop right now.)

Short forms: In speaking, we almost always use short forms: I'm eating, she's working, we're waiting. These are normal and natural.

2. Spelling Rules for Verb-ing

When to use it: You need to know these spelling rules to write verb-ing forms correctly.

Most verbs: add -ing.

  1. eat → eating.
  2. read → reading.
  3. work → working.

Verbs ending in -e: remove -e, add -ing.

  1. make → making.
  2. write → writing.
  3. live → living.

Short verbs ending in consonant-vowel-consonant: double the last letter, add -ing.

  1. sit → sitting.
  2. run → running.
  3. stop → stopping.

Remember: We do NOT double -w, -x, or -y. "Play" → "playing" (NOT "playying"). "Snow" → "snowing" (NOT "snowwing").

3. Negative Sentences

Form: subject + am / is / are + not + verb-ing

When to use it: Add "not" after "am/is/are" to make negative sentences.

  1. I am not sleeping. I am reading a book.
    (= I am not in bed.)
  2. He is not working today. He is at home.
    (= Today is his day off.)
  3. They are not staying at a hotel. They are staying with friends.
    (= Their friends have a house.)

Short forms:

  1. I'm not sleeping.
  2. He isn't working. / He's not working.
  3. They aren't staying. / They're not staying.

4. Questions

Form: am / is / are + subject + verb-ing + ?

When to use it: Put "am/is/are" before the subject to make questions.

  1. Are you waiting for a taxi?
    (= I want to know if you need a taxi.)
  2. Is she coming to the meeting?
    (= I want to know if she will be there.)
  3. What are they doing?
    (= I want to know their activity right now.)

Short answers:

  1. Are you leaving? — Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
  2. Is he working? — Yes, he is. / No, he isn't.
  3. Are they eating? — Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.

5. Present Simple vs Present Continuous

When to use it: This is one of the most confusing things for beginners. Here is the key difference.

Present simple = habits and routines (always, usually, every day):

  1. I drink coffee every morning.
    (= This is my habit.)

Present continuous = now, at this moment:

  1. I am drinking coffee right now.
    (= I have a cup in my hand.)

More examples:

  1. She works in a bank. (= her job, always) / She is working from home today. (= only today, now.)
  2. We usually take the bus. (= our routine) / We are taking a taxi today. (= this time, now.)

Native speaker note: Some verbs are NOT usually used in the continuous form. These are called "state verbs": like, love, hate, want, need, know, understand, believe. We say "I like coffee" (NOT "I am liking coffee").

Summary: Present Continuous

Type I he / she / it you / we / they
Positive I am eating. She is eating. They are eating.
Negative I'm not eating. She isn't eating. They aren't eating.
Question Am I eating? Is she eating? Are they eating?

Common Errors

Forgetting "be" (am/is/are)

She working now.

She is working now.

The present continuous always needs "am", "is", or "are" before the verb-ing form.

Using present continuous with state verbs

I am liking this food.

I like this food.

"Like" is a state verb. Use the present simple: I like, she likes. NOT "I am liking".

Wrong spelling of verb-ing

She is siting on the chair.

She is sitting on the chair.

"Sit" ends in consonant-vowel-consonant, so double the last letter: sitting.

How to Remember and Practice

📝 Tip: Right now, look around you. Describe what people are doing: "The man is reading. The woman is talking on the phone. The children are playing." This is the best way to practise the present continuous.

📝 Tip: Remember the key words: "right now", "at the moment", "today" = present continuous. "Every day", "always", "usually" = present simple.

📝 Tip: Learn the common state verbs that do NOT use the continuous form: like, love, hate, want, need, know, understand, believe, prefer, remember. These always use the present simple.
