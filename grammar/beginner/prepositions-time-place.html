Prepositions of Time and Place (in, on, at)

Introduction

"In", "on", and "at" are the three most important prepositions in English. You use them to talk about WHEN things happen (time) and WHERE things are (place). Choosing the right one can be confusing because the rules are different from many other languages. This lesson will give you clear, simple rules.

After this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Use "in", "on", and "at" correctly for time (months, days, hours).
  • Use "in", "on", and "at" correctly for place (countries, streets, addresses).
  • Remember the key patterns with a simple "big → medium → small" rule.

1. Prepositions of Time: "In", "On", "At"

The simple rule: Think from BIG to SMALL. In = long periods. On = specific days and dates. At = exact times and short moments.

IN = months, years, seasons, parts of the day:

  1. I was born in March.
    (= the month.)
  2. She started her job in 2023.
    (= the year.)
  3. It is very hot in summer.
    (= the season.)

ON = days and dates:

  1. I have a meeting on Monday.
    (= a specific day.)
  2. Her birthday is on 15th June.
    (= a specific date.)
  3. We don't work on weekends.
    (= Saturday and Sunday.)

AT = exact times and special moments:

  1. The train leaves at 9 o'clock.
    (= an exact time.)
  2. I eat lunch at noon.
    (= a specific point in the day.)
  3. We give presents at Christmas.
    (= a holiday period.)

Special cases: We say "at night" (NOT "in night"). We say "in the morning / in the afternoon / in the evening" but "at night".

2. Prepositions of Place: "In", "On", "At"

The simple rule: Again, think BIG to SMALL. In = inside something or large areas. On = surfaces and lines (streets, floors). At = specific points and locations.

IN = countries, cities, rooms, containers:

  1. I live in Brazil.
    (= a country.)
  2. She works in London.
    (= a city.)
  3. The milk is in the fridge.
    (= inside something.)

ON = streets, floors, surfaces:

  1. The shop is on King Street.
    (= a street.)
  2. My office is on the third floor.
    (= a floor of a building.)
  3. Your keys are on the table.
    (= a surface.)

AT = specific places and addresses:

  1. I am at the airport.
    (= a specific location.)
  2. She is at work.
    (= a specific place.)
  3. We met at the bus stop.
    (= a specific point.)

Common fixed phrases: at home, at work, at school, at the station, at the airport, at the door. These use "at" because they are specific points.

3. When NOT to Use a Preposition

When to use it: Do NOT use in/on/at before "this", "last", "next", "every", "yesterday", "today", "tomorrow".

  1. I saw her last week.
    (= NOT "in last week".)
  2. We have a meeting next Monday.
    (= NOT "on next Monday".)
  3. She goes swimming every Saturday.
    (= NOT "on every Saturday".)

Summary: In, On, At

Preposition Time Place
in months (in June), years (in 2024), seasons (in winter), parts of the day (in the morning). countries (in Japan), cities (in Paris), rooms (in the kitchen), containers (in the bag).
on days (on Monday), dates (on 5th March), weekends (on weekends). streets (on Park Street), floors (on the 2nd floor), surfaces (on the desk).
at exact times (at 3 pm), night (at night), mealtimes (at lunchtime), holidays (at Christmas). specific places (at the station), addresses (at 10 King Street), fixed phrases (at home, at work).

Common Errors

Using "in" for a specific day

The meeting is in Monday.

The meeting is on Monday.

Use "on" for days (Monday, Tuesday, etc.) and dates.

Using "on" for a time

The train leaves on 9 o'clock.

The train leaves at 9 o'clock.

Use "at" for exact times (9 o'clock, noon, midnight).

Using a preposition with "last/next/every"

I saw him on last Friday.

I saw him last Friday.

Do not use "in", "on", or "at" before "last", "next", "every", "this".

How to Remember and Practice

📝 Tip: Remember: BIG → in (months, years, countries, cities). MEDIUM → on (days, dates, streets, surfaces). SMALL → at (exact times, specific points).

📝 Tip: Learn the fixed phrases as a group: at home, at work, at school, at the airport, at the station. These never change.

📝 Tip: Write five sentences about your daily routine using prepositions of time: "I wake up at 7. I have a meeting on Wednesday. I go on holiday in August." Then check if you used in/on/at correctly.
