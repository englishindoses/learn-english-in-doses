Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Introduction

These two tenses talk about the present, but they are different. We use the present simple for things that are always true or happen regularly. We use the present continuous for things happening right now. Understanding the difference helps you describe your life and what is happening at this moment.

After this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Use the present simple to talk about habits and facts.
  • Use the present continuous to talk about actions happening now.
  • Choose the correct tense for different situations.

Present Simple: Form

Positive: subject + base verb (add -s/-es for he/she/it)

Negative: subject + don't/doesn't + base verb

Question: do/does + subject + base verb?

  1. I work in an office. (positive)
  2. She doesn't like coffee. (negative)
  3. Do you speak Spanish? (question)

Remember: For he/she/it, add -s or -es to the verb: work ‚Üí works, go ‚Üí goes, watch ‚Üí watches.

Present Simple: When to Use It

Use 1: Habits and routines - things you do regularly

  1. I drink tea every morning. ‚òï
  2. She goes to the gym on Mondays.
  3. We watch TV after dinner.

Use 2: Facts and things that are always true

  1. Water boils at 100 degrees.
  2. The sun rises in the east. üåÖ
  3. Cats sleep a lot.

Time words often used: always, usually, often, sometimes, never, every day/week/month, on Mondays

Present Continuous: Form

Positive: subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

Negative: subject + am not/isn't/aren't + verb-ing

Question: am/is/are + subject + verb-ing?

  1. I am working now. (positive)
  2. He isn't sleeping. (negative)
  3. Are you listening? (question)

Remember: Use am with I, is with he/she/it, and are with you/we/they.

Present Continuous: When to Use It

Use 1: Actions happening right now

  1. I am eating lunch. üçΩÔ∏è
  2. The baby is sleeping.
  3. They are playing football.

Use 2: Temporary situations - happening around now, but not permanent

  1. I am staying with my friend this week.
  2. She is learning English.
  3. He is working from home today.

Time words often used: now, right now, at the moment, today, this week, currently

Summary: Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Present Simple Present Continuous
Habits and routines Actions happening now
Facts (always true) Temporary situations
I work every day. I am working now.
She likes pizza. She is eating pizza.
always, usually, every day now, at the moment, today

Common Errors

1: Using present continuous for habits

I am going to work every day.


I go to work every day. ‚úì

Use present simple for things you do regularly.

2: Using present simple for actions happening now

Look! The baby sleeps.


Look! The baby is sleeping. ‚úì

Use present continuous for actions happening at this moment.

3: Forgetting the -s for he/she/it

She work in a hospital.


She works in a hospital. ‚úì

Remember to add -s/-es with he/she/it in present simple.

Useful Tips

üìù TIP 1: Look for time words! "Every day" = present simple. "Now" or "at the moment" = present continuous.

üìù TIP 2: Ask yourself: "Is this always true, or only now?" Always true = present simple. Only now = present continuous.

üìù TIP 3: Some verbs don't usually use the continuous form: like, love, know, understand, want, need. Say "I know" (not "I am knowing").
