Basic Sentence Structure & Word Order

English sentences follow a basic pattern that helps people understand your meaning. Learning this pattern will help you speak and write clearly from the beginning.

Form

Basic English Sentence Pattern:

Subject + Verb + Object (if needed)

I work in London.

She speaks English.

The train arrives at 9 o'clock.

When to Use This Pattern

1. Making Statements (Positive Sentences)

Use this pattern when you want to tell someone information or facts.

Examples in Daily Life:

  • At work: "I finish work at 5 o'clock."
  • At home: "My family lives in Manchester."
  • When travelling: "The hotel has a swimming pool."
  • About yourself: "I speak two languages."

Quick Practice: Put the words in the correct order

1. coffee / I / drink / every morning

2. the meeting / starts / at 10 o'clock

2. Making Questions

For questions, we change the word order slightly.

Question Pattern with 'be' verbs (am/is/are):

Am/Is/Are + Subject + Rest of sentence?

Are you from London?

Is the hotel near the airport?

Useful Questions for Daily Life:

  • At work: "Are you busy today?"
  • When travelling: "Is the train station far?"
  • Meeting people: "Are you from here?"

Quick Practice: Choose the correct question

1. You want to ask if someone is from Spain.

2. You want to ask if the meeting is important.
