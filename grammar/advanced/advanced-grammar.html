Advanced ESL Grammar Course

Welcome to the Advanced English Grammar Course by English in Doses. This course is designed to help you deepen your understanding of advanced grammar structures and refine your ability to express yourself clearly and confidently in English. Each module builds on your existing knowledge, guiding you through more complex structures and helping you become a more fluent, sophisticated user of the language.

This course will help you:

  • Express complex ideas more clearly and precisely.
  • Sound more natural and fluent.
  • Understand more sophisticated texts.
  • Get higher scores on exams like IELTS, TOEFL and other exams that require advanced grammatical knowledge (C1/C2).

Course Structure

Each topic includes:

  1. Introduction - Overview of the grammatical concept.
  2. Explanation - Structure, use and exceptions.
  3. Examples - Authentic usage in various contexts.
  4. Native Speaker Note - Quick notes about how native speakers use the grammar in real life.
  5. Common Errors - Typical mistakes and how to avoid them.
  6. Practice Exercises - Test your knowledge!
  7. Useful tips and advice - Extra tips about how to learn and use the grammar.
  8. Extra Practice - After each topic you can access multiple, short extra practice activities to practice what you have learned.

Course Index

The topics are in order from easiest to hardest. Follow them in order and progress through, or look for specific topics you want to work on. Tick the checkboxes to track your progress!

1. Advanced Tenses and Aspect

  • Present Perfect Progressive vs. Present Perfect Simple
  • Past Perfect Progressive vs. Past Perfect
  • Future Perfect vs. Future Perfect Continuous
  • Mixed Time Frames with Perfect Tenses
  • Advanced Uses of Present Perfect
  • Perfect Infinitives and Perfect Gerunds
  • Narrative Tenses (Past Perfect, Past Simple, Past Continuous)

2. Conditionals and Hypotheticals

  • Zero, First, Second, Third, and Mixed Conditionals
  • Inverted Conditionals (e.g., "Had I known...")
  • Conditional Expressions (Unless, Provided that, As long as)
  • Wish, If only, and Would rather for regret and desire

  • Mixed Modal Constructions
