Were-Subjunctive and Past Perfect Subjunctive

Introduction

The were-subjunctive and past perfect subjunctive are special verb forms used to talk about hypothetical and unreal situations. These forms help you express wishes, give advice, and imagine different possibilities. They are commonly used in everyday English and make your language sound more natural and fluent.

After this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Use "were" correctly for all subjects in hypothetical situations and wishes about the present.
  • Form the past perfect subjunctive to express regrets and imagine different past outcomes.
  • Recognise when to use each form based on whether you're talking about the present or the past.

Were-Subjunctive (Hypothetical Present/Future)

Form: if/wish/as if + subject + were (for all subjects, including I, he, she, it)

When to use it: Use "were" (not "was") for all subjects when talking about hypothetical or unreal situations in the present or future. This form appears after if, wish, as if, as though, and suppose.

Common patterns:

  • If I/he/she/it were... (hypothetical conditionals)
  • I wish I/he/she/it were... (wishes about present)
  • He acts as if he were... (imaginary comparisons)
  1. If I were you, I would accept the job. (not "was" - giving advice)
  2. I wish she were here right now. (not "was" - impossible wish)
  3. He speaks as if he were an expert. (not "was" - he isn't really an expert)

Native speaker note: In informal speech, many native speakers use "was" instead of "were" with I, he, she, and it ("If I was you..."). However, in formal writing, exams, and careful speech, always use "were" for hypothetical situations.

Past Perfect Subjunctive (Hypothetical Past)

Form: if/wish/if only + subject + had + past participle (V3)

When to use it: Use the past perfect subjunctive to talk about hypothetical or unreal situations in the past. This expresses regret or imagines how the past could have been different.

Common patterns:

  • If + subject + had + past participle (V3)... (past hypothetical conditionals)
  • I wish + subject + had + past participle (V3)... (regrets about the past)
  • If only + subject + had + past participle (V3)... (strong regrets)
  1. If I had known about the problem, I would have helped. (I didn't know, so I didn't help)
  2. I wish I had studied harder for the exam. (I didn't study hard enough - regret)
  3. If only they had arrived five minutes earlier. (they didn't arrive earlier - regret)

Remember: The past perfect subjunctive always refers to the past, not the present. Compare: "I wish I were rich" (present wish) vs "I wish I had been rich" (past regret).

Summary: Were-Subjunctive vs Past Perfect Subjunctive

Type Form Time Reference Example
Were-subjunctive if/as if/wish + subject + were Present/future (hypothetical) If I were rich, I would travel more.
Past perfect subjunctive if/if only/wish + subject + had + V3 Past (unreal past) I wish I had known earlier.

Common Errors

1: Using "was" instead of "were" in hypothetical situations

If I was you, I would accept the offer.


If I were you, I would accept the offer. ‚úì

Use "were" (not "was") for all subjects when talking about hypothetical or unreal situations.

2: Using simple past instead of past perfect for past regrets

I wish I studied harder last year.


I wish I had studied harder last year. ‚úì

To express regret about the past, use "had + past participle (V3)", not simple past.

3: Confusing present and past wishes

I wish I had more time right now. (incorrect form)


I wish I had more time right now. ‚úì (use simple past for present wishes)

I wish I were richer right now. ‚úì (use "were" for the verb "be")

For wishes about the present, use simple past or "were". For wishes about the past, use "had + V3".

Useful Tips

üìù TIP 1: For hypothetical situations with if or wish, always use "were" for ALL subjects (I, you, he, she, it, we, they). This is the safe formal choice.

üìù TIP 2: Time markers help you choose the right form: "right now/at the moment" = were-subjunctive; "yesterday/last year" = past perfect subjunctive.

üìù TIP 3: Think about reality: If something is impossible or untrue NOW, use "were". If something didn't happen in the PAST, use "had + V3".
