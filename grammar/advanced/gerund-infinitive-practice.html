Gerund vs Infinitive: Reference Sheet

Quick Reference Guide

Use this table as a quick reminder of the meaning changes when these verbs are followed by gerund (verb-ing) or infinitive (to + verb).

Verb + verb-ing + to-infinitive
Stop Finish/quit an activity.
He stopped smoking.		 Pause to do something else.
He stopped to smoke.
Remember Have a memory of a past action.
I remember meeting her.		 Not forget to do a task.
Remember to call me.
Forget Lose the memory of something (usually with "never").
I'll never forget visiting Rome.		 Fail to do a task.
I forgot to lock the door.
Try Experiment with a solution.
Try restarting the computer.		 Make an effort to do something difficult.
I tried to open the door.
Regret Feel sorry about a past action.
I regret telling her the secret.		 Feel sorry about current news (formal).
I regret to inform you...
Go on Continue the same activity.
She went on talking for hours.		 Move to a different activity.
She went on to become CEO.
Mean Involve/result in something.
This job means working late.		 Intend/plan to do something.
I didn't mean to hurt you.
Need Require an action (passive meaning).
The car needs washing.		 Must do something (active meaning).
I need to wash the car.

Quick Tips

üìå STOP: "Did the activity end?" ‚Üí use verb-ing. "Did I pause to do something?" ‚Üí use to-infinitive.

üìå REMEMBER/FORGET: Gerund = past (a memory). Infinitive = future (a task to do).

üìå TRY: Verb-ing = suggestion/experiment. To-infinitive = difficult attempt.

üìå NEED: "The car needs washing" = passive (the car is washed). "I need to wash the car" = active (I wash it).
