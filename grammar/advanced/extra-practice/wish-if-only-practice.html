Multiple Choice Questions

Choose the correct option to complete each sentence.

1. I wish I _____ able to speak Spanish.

A. am
B. were
C. would be
D. will be

2. If only we _____ to the beach yesterday instead of staying home.

A. go
B. went
C. had gone
D. would go

3. I would rather _____ at home than go to the party tonight.

A. stay
B. staying
C. stayed
D. to stay

4. She wishes she _____ the job offer when she had the chance.

A. accepts
B. accepted
C. had accepted
D. would accept

5. I'd rather you _____ tell anyone about this problem yet.

A. don't
B. didn't
C. won't
D. not to

6. If only it _____ raining soon, I want to go for a walk.

A. stops
B. stopped
C. would stop
D. had stopped

7. I wish it _____ so cold today. I'm freezing!

A. isn't
B. wasn't
C. wouldn't be
D. hadn't been

8. I would rather _____ by train yesterday, but all tickets were sold out.

A. traveled
B. travel
C. have traveled
D. had traveled

9. Which is grammatically correct for present wishes?

A. I wish I have more time.
B. I wish I had more time.
C. I wish I will have more time.
D. I wish I would have more time.

10. Which of these expresses a preference for others' actions?

A. I would rather stay at home.
B. I would rather have gone earlier.
C. I'd rather you didn't smoke in here.
D. I'd rather not go to the cinema.

Matching Exercise

Match each expression with its correct form or meaning.

I wish I were taller.
Present wish/regret (I'm not tall now)
I wish I had studied medicine.
Past regret (I didn't study medicine)
I wish it would stop raining.
Desire for future change (it's raining now)
I would rather stay at home.
Present/future preference for yourself
I would rather have gone by train.
Past preference for yourself
I'd rather you didn't tell anyone.
Present/future preference about others
If only I could speak Japanese.
More emphatic version of present wish
If only they had arrived earlier.
More emphatic version of past regret
