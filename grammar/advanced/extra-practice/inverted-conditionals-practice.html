Multiple Choice Questions

Choose the correct inverted conditional form for each sentence.

1. "If I were you, I would accept the job offer." The inverted form is:

A. Were I you, I would accept the job offer.
B. Were I you, I will accept the job offer.
C. I were you, I would accept the job offer.
D. Were you I, I would accept the job offer.

2. "If she had told me earlier, I would have helped her." The inverted form is:

A. Had she told me earlier, I would have helped her.
B. Had she told earlier me, I would have helped her.
C. She had told me earlier, I would have helped her.
D. Had told she me earlier, I would have helped her.

3. "If you should have any questions, please call this number." The inverted form is:

A. Should you have any questions, please call this number.
B. You should have any questions, please call this number.
C. Should any questions you have, please call this number.
D. If should you have any questions, please call this number.

4. "If it were not for your help, I would have failed the exam." The inverted form is:

A. Were it for your help, I would have failed the exam.
B. Were it not for your help, I would have failed the exam.
C. Were not it for your help, I would have failed the exam.
D. Not were it for your help, I would have failed the exam.

5. Which of these is a correctly formed negative inverted conditional?

A. Had not I studied, I would have failed.
B. Not had I studied, I would have failed.
C. Had I not studied, I would have failed.
D. If had I not studied, I would have failed.

6. "If I were to win the lottery, I would buy a house." The inverted form is:

A. Were I to win the lottery, I would buy a house.
B. Were to I win the lottery, I would buy a house.
C. Were I win the lottery, I would buy a house.
D. I were to win the lottery, I would buy a house.

7. What is the correct inverted form of "If they had arrived on time, they would have seen the performance"?

A. Arrived had they on time, they would have seen the performance.
B. Had they on time arrived, they would have seen the performance.
C. Had they arrived on time, they would have seen the performance.
D. They had arrived on time, they would have seen the performance.

8. "If we were to know the truth, we might be shocked." The inverted form is:

A. Were we to know the truth, we might be shocked.
B. Were we know the truth, we might be shocked.
C. We were to know the truth, we might be shocked.
D. Were to we know the truth, we might be shocked.

9. Which of these is NOT a correctly formed inverted conditional?

A. Were she to arrive early, she would get a good seat.
B. Had I known about the party, I would have attended.
C. Should you need help, please ask the staff.
D. If would I have studied, I would have passed.

10. "If you could change one thing about your past, what would it be?" The inverted form is:

A. Could you change one thing about your past, what would it be?
B. You could change one thing about your past, what would it be?
C. Could change you one thing about your past, what would it be?
D. If could you change one thing about your past, what would it be?
