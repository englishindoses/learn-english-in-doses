Gerund vs Infinitive: Quick Reference

About This Reference Sheet

Some verbs in English can be followed by either a gerund (verb-ing) or an infinitive (to + verb), but the meaning changes depending on which form you use.

This reference sheet summarises the seven most important verbs where the choice between gerund and infinitive creates a difference in meaning. Keep this handy when writing or speaking to help you choose the correct form.

Key Points to Remember

  • Gerund (verb-ing) often refers to past or general actions, experiences, or what something involves.
  • Infinitive (to + verb) often refers to future actions, intentions, or purposes.
  • Choosing the wrong form can completely change your meaning, so pay attention to context.
Verb + verb-ing + to-infinitive
Stop Finish or quit an activity. He stopped smoking last year. Pause to do something else. He stopped to smoke outside.
Remember Have a memory of a past action. I remember meeting him at the party. Not forget to do a future task. Remember to lock the door.
Forget Have a memory of a past experience (often with "never"). I'll never forget visiting Paris. Fail to do something you needed to do. I forgot to buy milk.
Try Experiment with something as a solution. Try adding more salt to the soup. Make an effort or attempt (something difficult). I tried to open the window but it was stuck.
Regret Feel sorry about a past action. I regret telling her the secret. Feel sorry about giving bad news (formal). We regret to inform you that your application was unsuccessful.
Go on Continue the same activity. She went on talking for hours. Move to a different activity or stage. After university, she went on to become a doctor.
Mean Involve or result in something. This job means working weekends. Intend or plan to do something. I didn't mean to hurt you.
Need Something requires an action (passive meaning). The car needs washing. (= needs to be washed) Someone must do something (active meaning). I need to wash the car.

üí° Quick Tips

  • Remember/Forget: Think about time. verb-ing = past memory. to-infinitive = future task.
  • Stop: "Stop + verb-ing" = quit completely. "Stop + to-infinitive" = pause for a purpose.
  • Try: "Try + verb-ing" = experiment. "Try + to-infinitive" = make an effort.
  • Need: "Need + verb-ing" has a passive meaning. The subject receives the action.
