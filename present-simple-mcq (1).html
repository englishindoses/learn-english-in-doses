Introduction

This quiz will test your understanding of the present simple tense in English. Choose the best answer for each question.

The present simple tense is used for:

  • Habits and routines (I walk to work every day)
  • Permanent situations (She lives in London)
  • General truths and facts (Water boils at 100¬∞C)
  • Timetables and schedules (The train leaves at 6 pm)

Present Simple Rules

Form for I, you, we, they: Subject + base verb (infinitive without 'to')

  1. I work in a hospital. (Positive)
  2. We don't live in London. (Negative)
  3. Do you speak English? (Question)

Form for he, she, it: Subject + base verb + -s/-es

  1. She works in a school. (Positive)
  2. He doesn't play tennis. (Negative)
  3. Does it rain a lot here? (Question)

Remember: We use the present simple for habits, routines, facts, and scheduled events - not for actions happening right now.
