Lesson 4: Baggage Claim and Problems

Reporting Lost or Damaged Luggage

Lesson Goal

Find your luggage at baggage claim, report problems, and describe your bag.

What you will learn:

  • Vocabulary for baggage claim and reporting problems.
  • How to report lost or damaged luggage using past simple negative.
  • How to describe your bag (colour, size, and details).

Grammar: Past Simple Negative

This is how we form the simple past negative. We use didn't (did not) + the base form of the verb.

Form: subject + didn't + verb (base form)

  1. I didn't see him at the party. (daily life)
  2. She didn't finish her report yesterday. (work)
  3. The train didn't arrive on time. (travel)

Remember: After didn't, always use the base form of the verb. Say "didn't arrive" (NOT "didn't arrived").

Common Sentences at Baggage Claim

  1. My bag didn't arrive.
  2. My suitcase didn't come out on the carousel.
  3. I didn't get my luggage.
  4. The airline didn't call me.
  5. They didn't find my bag.

üé§ Now It's Your Turn to Speak!

You've completed all the activities. Use these conversations to practise speaking with your teacher or a partner.

Speaking Practice

Practise these conversations with your teacher or a partner.

Conversation 1: Finding Baggage Claim

You: Excuse me, where is baggage claim?

Staff: Go downstairs and follow the signs. It's on the left.

You: Which carousel is for flight BA246?

Staff: Carousel 5.

You: Thank you!

Conversation 2: Reporting Lost Luggage

You: Excuse me, my bag didn't arrive.

Staff: I'm sorry to hear that. What flight were you on?

You: Flight BA246 from S√£o Paulo.

Staff: Can you describe your bag?

You: It's a big blue suitcase with wheels. It has a yellow tag.

Staff: Okay. Please fill out this claim form.

You: When will my bag arrive?

Staff: We will call you when we find it. Here is your reference number.

You: Thank you.

Conversation 3: Reporting Damaged Luggage

You: Excuse me, my suitcase is damaged.

Staff: I'm sorry. What's the problem?

You: The wheel is broken.

Staff: Can I see your boarding pass and luggage tag?

You: Yes, here they are.

Staff: Please fill out this damage report form. We will contact you about compensation.

You: How long will that take?

Staff: About two weeks. Here is your reference number.

Role Play Scenarios

Try these situations with your teacher or a partner.

Scenario Student A (Traveller) Student B (Staff)
1. Lost Bag Your bag didn't arrive. It's a medium black suitcase with a green tag. You are staying at the Grand Hotel. Ask for flight number, description of the bag, and delivery address. Give a reference number.
2. Damaged Bag Your bag arrived but it's damaged. The handle is broken. You want compensation. Ask to see the damage and the luggage tag. Give a damage report form.
3. Describing Luggage Your bag is lost. Describe it: big, red, with wheels, has a white ribbon on the handle, brand is "Travel Pro". Ask questions about size, colour, brand, and any special details. Fill out the claim form.

Remember: Use "My bag didn't arrive" for lost luggage, "My bag is damaged" for broken bags, and describe your bag with size + colour + details (e.g., "It's a big black suitcase with wheels").
