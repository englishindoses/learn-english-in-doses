Target Language: "I'd like..."

When you check in at a hotel, you need to ask for things politely. We use "I'd like..." to sound polite.

Meaning

"I'd like" is a polite way to say "I want". We use it with hotel staff, waiters, shop assistants, and people we don't know.

"I'd like" is the short form of "I would like".

Form

There are two patterns. You can use "I'd like" with a noun, or with to + verb.

Pattern 1: i'd like + a / an + noun

  1. I'd like a non-smoking room.
  2. I'd like a room with a view.
  3. I'd like a key card, please.

Pattern 2: i'd like + to + verb

  1. I'd like to check in, please.
  2. I'd like to book a taxi.
  3. I'd like to pay by card.

Careful! Don't forget "to" before the verb.

✅ I'd like to check in.

❌ I'd like check in.

Tip: Add "please" at the end to sound even more polite.

Guided Practice

Read the model conversation first. Then use the cue cards to role-play with your partner.

Model Conversation

Receptionist: Good afternoon! Welcome to the Sunshine Hotel.

Guest: Hello. I'd like to check in, please.

Receptionist: Do you have a reservation?

Guest: Yes, the name is Silva.

Receptionist: Ah yes, Mr Silva. A room for three nights.

Guest: That's right. I'd like a non-smoking room, please.

Receptionist: Of course. Here's your key card. Your room number is 502, on the fifth floor.

Guest: Thank you. What time is breakfast?

Receptionist: Breakfast is from 7 to 10am.

Guest: And what's the WiFi password?

Receptionist: It's SUNSHINE2026.

Guest: Perfect. Thank you very much!

Now it's your turn! Role-play with the cue cards.

Free Production

Now try a real check-in! No cue cards, no script. Speak naturally.

How to play:

  1. Your teacher is the receptionist. You are the guest.
  2. Choose a situation from the list below (or make your own).
  3. Check in! Ask questions. Ask for what you want.
  4. Then swap: you are the receptionist and your teacher is the guest.

Choose your situation:

  1. You are travelling with your family. You want a big room.
  2. You are on a business trip. You want a quiet room on a high floor.
  3. You are on holiday with your partner. You want a room with a beautiful view.
  4. You are travelling alone. You want a small, cheap room.
  5. You forgot your reservation number! Try to check in without it.

Try to use: "I'd like a..." + "I'd like to..." + questions about breakfast, WiFi, check-out time, and the lift.

Wrap-up & Review

Quick recap

  1. I'd like + a + noun → I'd like a non-smoking room.
  2. I'd like + to + verb → I'd like to check in.
  3. Add "please" to be extra polite.

Common mistakes to check

Which sentence is correct?

❌ I like a room with a view.

✅ I'd like a room with a view.
(We say "I'd like", not "I like", for polite requests.)


❌ I'd like check in, please.

✅ I'd like to check in, please.
(Don't forget "to" before the verb.)
