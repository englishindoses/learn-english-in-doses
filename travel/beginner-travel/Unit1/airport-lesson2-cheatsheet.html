Grammar: Questions with “What” and “Where”
Question word Pattern Example
What time + is/are + noun? What time is boarding?
What time + do/does + subject + verb? What time do we land?
What + is/are + noun? What are the drink options?
Where + is/are + noun? Where is the emergency exit?
Where + can + subject + verb? Where can I put my bag?
Key Phrases

Asking for something

Can I have...?

Can I have a blanket, please?

Use this to request something politely

Asking about time

What time...?

What time do we arrive?

Use this to ask about times

Asking for location

Where is...?

Where is the emergency exit?

Use this to find places or things

Getting attention

Excuse me...

Excuse me, can I have some water?

Use this before asking for help

Model Conversations

Asking for Comfort Items

Passenger: Excuse me.

FA: Yes, how can I help you?

Passenger: Can I have a blanket, please?

FA: Of course. Here you are.

Passenger: And can I have some water?

FA: Still or sparkling?

Passenger: Still, please.

Asking About the Flight

Passenger: Excuse me, what time do we arrive?

FA: We arrive at 3:30 local time.

Passenger: And what time is the meal?

FA: We serve lunch in about one hour.

Passenger: Great, thank you.

Finding Things on the Plane

Passenger: Excuse me, where is the emergency exit?

FA: It's at the back, behind row 25.

Passenger: And where can I put my bag?

FA: In the overhead bin, or under the seat in front.

Remember!

  • "Can I have...?" — for polite requests (a blanket, some water, a pillow)
  • "What time...?" — for asking about time (do we land, is the meal)
  • "Where is...?" — for asking about location (singular). Use "Where are...?" for plural
  • "Excuse me" — before asking a flight attendant for help
