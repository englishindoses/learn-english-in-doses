Lesson 5: Reporting Luggage Problems

Communicating About Missing and Damaged Luggage

Lesson Goal

Report missing or damaged luggage and understand what happens next.

What you will learn:

  • Vocabulary for suitcase parts, damage, and reporting problems.
  • How to use "will" to ask and answer questions about your luggage.
  • How to describe damage to your bag and ask for help.

Grammar: Will (Future)

We use will to talk about the future. At baggage claim, you use "will" to ask questions and understand what will happen next.

Positive (+)

Form: subject + will + verb (base form)

  1. They will deliver your bag to the hotel.
  2. We will call you when we find it.
  3. The airline will contact you.

Negative (−)

Form: subject + will not (won't) + verb (base form)

  1. Your bag won't arrive today.
  2. They won't deliver it before tomorrow.
  3. The airline won't call you tonight.

Tip: "Won't" is the short form of "will not". In conversation, we almost always say "won't".

Questions (?)

Form: Will + subject + verb (base form)?

For "when" and "how long" questions: question word + will + subject + verb?

  1. Will you deliver it to my hotel?
  2. Will I get compensation?
  3. When will my bag arrive?

Remember: After "will" or "won't", always use the base form of the verb. Say "will arrive" (NOT "will arrives").

Short Answers

When someone asks a "will" question, you can give a short answer.

  1. Will you deliver my bag? — Yes, we will. / No, we won't.
  2. Will I get compensation? — Yes, you will. / No, you won't.
  3. Will they call me? — Yes, they will. / No, they won't.

🎤 Now It's Your Turn to Speak!

You've completed all the activities. Use these conversations to practise speaking with your teacher or a partner.

Speaking Practice

Practise these conversations with your teacher or a partner.

Conversation 1: Missing Luggage

You: Excuse me, my bag didn't arrive.

Staff: I'm sorry. What flight were you on?

You: Flight BA246 from São Paulo.

Staff: Can you describe your bag?

You: It's a big blue suitcase with wheels.

Staff: Please fill out this claim form.

You: When will my bag arrive?

Staff: We will call you when we find it.

You: Will you deliver it to my hotel?

Staff: Yes, we will. Where are you staying?

You: I'm staying at the Grand Hotel.

Staff: Here is your reference number.

You: Thank you.

Conversation 2: Damaged Luggage

You: Excuse me, my suitcase is damaged.

Staff: I'm sorry. What is the problem?

You: The handle is broken and the zipper is damaged.

Staff: Can I see your boarding pass and luggage tag?

You: Yes, here they are.

Staff: Please fill out this form.

You: Will I get compensation?

Staff: Yes, you will. We will contact you.

You: How long will it take?

Staff: About two weeks. Here is your reference number.

You: Thank you.

Role Play Scenarios

Try these situations with your teacher or a partner.

Scenario Student A (Traveller) Student B (Staff)
1. Missing Bag Your bag didn't arrive. It's a medium black suitcase. You are staying at the Park Hotel. Ask when your bag will arrive and if they will deliver it. Ask for a description of the bag. Give a claim form to fill out. Give a reference number. Say you will call when you find the bag.
2. Damaged Bag Your bag arrived but the wheel is broken. Ask if you will get compensation and how long it will take. Ask to see the damage. Give a form to fill out. Say you will contact them in two weeks. Give a reference number.
3. Damaged and Late Your bag arrived late and the handle is broken and the zipper is damaged. You want compensation. You are staying at the City Hotel. Ask about the damage. Give a form to fill out. Say you will deliver compensation information to their hotel. Give a reference number.

Remember: Use "My bag didn't arrive" for missing luggage, "The [part] is broken/damaged" for damaged bags, and "Will you...?" or "When will...?" to ask about what happens next.
