Key Phrases

Polite request

I'd like to...

I'd like to check in, please.

Use this to ask for something politely

Asking for location

Where is...?

Where is gate 15?

Use this to find places

Asking for something

Can I have...?

Can I have a window seat?

Use this to request something

Getting attention

Excuse me...

Excuse me, where is terminal 2?

Use this before asking for help

At Security — Common Instructions
"Take off your belt."
"Remove your laptop from your bag."
"Put your liquids in a clear plastic bag."
"Take off your shoes."
Model Conversations

At Check-in

You: I'd like to check in for my flight to Paris.

Staff: Can I have your passport, please?

You: Here you are. Can I have a window seat?

Staff: Of course. Here is your boarding pass.

At Security

Staff: Please take off your belt and shoes.

You: Do I need to remove my laptop?

Staff: Yes, please put it in a separate tray.

Finding Your Gate

You: Excuse me, where is gate B15?

Staff: Go straight and turn right. It's at the end.

You: Thank you!

Remember!

  • "I'd like to..." — for polite requests
  • "Where is...?" — for finding locations
  • "Can I have...?" — for asking for things
  • "Excuse me" — before asking for help
